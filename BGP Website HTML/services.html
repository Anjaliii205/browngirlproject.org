For Organizations

Listening Labs.

Structured, facilitated small-group conversations that generate qualitative research insights while creating genuine value for participants.

Designed for organizations that want to understand their communities — not just survey them. Participants leave feeling heard, not processed.

The Process

Three phases.

01

Co-Design

We define the research question together. The focus emerges from your goals and your community's priorities — not a generic template.

02

Facilitation

Small groups of 4–8 participants. Trauma-informed facilitation that creates real space for honest reflection — not just answers people think you want to hear.

03

Analysis & Return

Findings developed through qualitative thematic analysis, delivered in accessible format, and shared back with participants before broader publication.

What You Receive

Everything documented.

Facilitated session(s) with your target community
Full thematic analysis of findings
Written report in accessible language
Executive summary for internal or funder use
Recommendations for program design or next steps
Optional: return-of-findings session with participants
Services

Who This Is For

Organizations working with communities.

Nonprofits, NGOs, foundations, and academic institutions that work with communities and need grounded insight — not assumptions. Organizations designing programs for women, communities of color, or immigrant populations. Teams that want participatory methods without building internal research capacity.
